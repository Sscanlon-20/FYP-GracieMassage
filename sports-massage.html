What is Sports Massage?

Sports Massage does not have to be saved just for treatment of injuries that have already occurred, it is much more powerful as a preventative tool to keep the body healthy. This Massage is tailored to each individual's needs, using different sports massage techniques

What is it used for?

  • Helps break down scar tissue
  • Realigns adhesion's
  • Will help lengthen or shorten tissues

Techniques Used in Sports Massage

  • Effleurage-Flat and relaxed hand - Generally done at the beginning and ending of massage. Helps warm the skin and prepare for deeper work.
  • Petrissage a compression stroke - Such as kneading to help break down tension, improve elasticity in the muscle, aids relaxation.
  • Frictions - A very deep technique, heavily stimulating on the blood flow.
  • Circular - Helps with lubrication of joint membrane.
  • Percussions - These are invigorating strokes which will stimulate a muscle and mainly used over weekend atrophied muscles.
  • Vibrations - A shake to the muscle to aid with relaxation, loosen adhesions, generally used on calves and quadriceps.
  • Neuromuscular techniques - This provokes a reflex in the nervous system, helps muscle to relax, reduce spasms and pain.
  • Soft tissue release - Technique that improves flexibility in a localised area of muscle or tendon.
  • Muscle energy technique - Isometric contraction followed by a stretch, This technique inhibits the muscles spindles and Golgi tendon organs to allow an improvement in flexibility.
  • Manual Lymphatic drainage - To encourage deep and superficial lymphatic drainage, light deep strokes working towards the heart.
