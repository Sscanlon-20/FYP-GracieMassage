What is Sports Massage?

Sports Massage is a specialised therapeutic approach geared towards injury prevention and treatment, offering personalized care tailored to individual needs, whether for athletes or active individuals. It encompasses various techniques such as deep tissue massage, Swedish massage, stretching, and trigger point therapy to alleviate muscle tension, enhance relaxation, and improve flexibility. This form of massage not only helps prevent injuries by addressing areas of tension and promoting optimal muscle function but also contributes to overall physical health and well-being. Regular sessions can lead to improved athletic performance, quicker recovery from workouts, reduced stress levels, and better posture. Whether you're a professional athlete striving for peak performance or someone who enjoys regular physical activity, incorporating sports massage into your wellness routine can be highly beneficial. It serves as a proactive measure to maintain optimal physical health and minimize the risk of injuries, ensuring you can continue to pursue your fitness goals with confidence.

What is it used for?

  • Breaks down scar tissue
  • Realigns adhesions
  • Lengthens or shortens tissues
  • Improves circulation
  • Reduces muscle soreness and tension
  • Enhances athletic performance
  • Facilitates muscle recovery
  • Promotes relaxation and reduces stress levels

Techniques Used in Sports Massage

  • Effleurage-Flat and relaxed hand - Generally done at the beginning and ending of massage. Helps warm the skin and prepare for deeper work.
  • Petrissage a compression stroke - Such as kneading to help break down tension, improve elasticity in the muscle, aids relaxation.
  • Frictions - A very deep technique, heavily stimulating on the blood flow.
  • Circular - Helps with lubrication of joint membrane.
  • Percussions - These are invigorating strokes which will stimulate a muscle and mainly used over weekend atrophied muscles.
  • Vibrations - A shake to the muscle to aid with relaxation, loosen adhesions, generally used on calves and quadriceps.
  • Neuromuscular techniques - This provokes a reflex in the nervous system, helps muscle to relax, reduce spasms and pain.
  • Soft tissue release - Technique that improves flexibility in a localised area of muscle or tendon.
  • Muscle energy technique - Isometric contraction followed by a stretch, This technique inhibits the muscles spindles and Golgi tendon organs to allow an improvement in flexibility.
  • Manual Lymphatic drainage - To encourage deep and superficial lymphatic drainage, light deep strokes working towards the heart.
